02 · WHAT THIS BOOK IS ABOUT

I became very good at surviving a life I could no longer live.

A memoir, living archive, and field guide to the life that remained.

Now That I’m Still Here is a memoir about autism, masking, emotional abuse, psychiatric collapse, grief, and the strange work of rebuilding a life after the version of you everyone applauded finally breaks.

I grew up between countries, languages, churches, and expectations, learning early that belonging often meant performing. By adulthood, I could read a room, manage a crisis, and disappear in plain sight. People called it maturity. I was slowly evacuating my own personality.

Then the marriage collapsed. The mask cracked. I was diagnosed with autism as an adult. In the wreckage, Shadow kept expecting breakfast, Katie loved me without asking me to become smaller, and writing became a door.

This is not a victory lap. It is what happens after collapse, when you are alive but not yet convinced that life belongs to you, and the people who stay make staying imaginable.

The book is not about becoming fearless. It is about becoming present enough to stop disappearing.
