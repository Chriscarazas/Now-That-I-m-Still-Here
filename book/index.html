The Memoir

NOW THAT I’M
STILL HERE

Now That I’m Still Here is Christopher Carazas’s memoir about late-diagnosed autism, psychological abuse, grief, survival, belonging, and Shadow, the dog who helped him stay.

A memoir without the inspirational varnish.

Christopher grew up autistic across five countries without knowing it. By adulthood, he could become whoever the room required, except himself. A late diagnosis explained the pattern. Psychological abuse nearly erased what remained. Grief arrived before the rebuilding was finished.

This is the honest version of survival before anyone turns it into a quote over a mountain.
