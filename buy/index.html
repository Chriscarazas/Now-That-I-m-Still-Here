BEFORE YOU PURCHASE A 204-PAGE EMOTIONAL AMBUSH

What, precisely, are you buying?

This is a memoir about late-diagnosed autism, masking, emotional abuse, psychiatric collapse, grief, survival, and the deeply inconvenient discovery that being alive is not the same thing as having a life.

It includes family silence, religious disappointment, several continents, one devastating love story, one German Shepherd operating without meaningful oversight, and the bureaucratic miracle of remaining alive long enough to fill out another intake form.

It is sad. It is funny. It is occasionally furious. It contains no five-step healing plan because suffering, rudely, declined to organize itself into a webinar.

THE PHYSICAL OBJECT

Ten copies. One witness. Several routes into the same story.

The book is not a mockup here. It has weight, edges, shipping labels, and a German Shepherd declining to participate in inventory control.
